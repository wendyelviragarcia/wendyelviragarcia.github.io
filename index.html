Elvira-García

Assistant Professor | Serra Húnter Fellow

Wendy Elvira-García is an assistant professor (Serra Húnter tenure-track program) working at the University of Barcelona (Spain), member of the Phonetics Laboratory, academic coordinator of the Cognitive Science and Language master program and associate editor of Estudios de Fonética Experimental.

She is author and coauthor for 27 research papers published in peer-reviewed journals (19 JCR), a book review and more than 60 congress presentations. In addition to this, she has published 2 books (1 as sole author, 1 coauthored), coauthored 16 book chapters, and edited two books. She has been keynote speaker in congresses 5 times and been invited to give conferences in universities from 5 countries.

Her research interests are both theoretical and applied phonetics.

Her theoretical research deals with the syntax/pragmatics-prosody interface from the perspective of construction grammar and particularly the prosody of insubordination. In applied phonetics, she works mainly programming tools that can be used in clinical and forensic acoustic analysis. She is particularly proud of an automatic transcriber of Spanish and Catalan intonation and a software for calculating prosodic distances among different linguistic varieties (dialectometry).

She has previously worked as postdoc researcher "Juan de la Cierva" in the Universidad Nacional de Educación a Distancia, as Phonetics lecturer (adjunct) in the Universitat de Barcelona (Spain), Universitat Pompeu Fabra (Spain) and Universität Onsnabrück (Germany) and as invited professor in he Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile (Chile).

She has taught multiple subjects dealing with Phonetics and Corpus Linguistics and she has been the PI of a teaching innovation project for improving the Spanish Phonetics course in the UNED.

Research projects

CORPAT

CORPAT - PEPLEs (Corpus digital para la preservación y el estudio del patrimonio lingüístico del español) (TED2021-130752A-I00) is a tool for the unified search of Peninsular Spanish and Canarian Regional Corpus.

  • Webpage

ProA

ProA is a project for the development of prosody assessment materials, guidelines and development of automatic assessment tools.

  • Webpage

Chiara VR

Chiara is a virtual reality app for improving public speeches. I have worked developing the automatic assessment on intonation, loudness and speech rate of the program.

  • App webpage

AMPER

AMPER stands for Atlas Multimedia del Espacio Románico, an international project aimed at gathering prosodic data of Romance languages. FFI2015-64859-P
